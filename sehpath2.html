E-Teaching Efficiently

Project Scope:

  • My Role: User Research, System Thinking, Wireframes
  • User Base: School Teachers
  • Tools: Sketch, Pen and Paper
  • Timeline: 30 July- 2 August 2020 (4 days)
  • Team: Me, myself and I

With the newfound need for learning online, teachers and students are adapting to the tools at hand. Schools are investing in upgrading the tools of their educators.

The Problem

With the sudden unprepared onset of e-learning, there is a massive disconnect between teachers and students, and also with collaboration amongst teachers.
With the existing system being majorly student-centric, we should focus on a Learning Management System to help teachers with real-time insights.

Brief

To understand the roles and methods of educators and supplement them to enhance teaching quality in classrooms.

User Interviews

I interviewed teachers, students and parents to understand the current methods and implications.

  • What is the current method of engaging students?
  • Is there a way for teachers to give feedback on assignments promptly and effectively?
  • What tools are used for drafting and curating lesson plans?
  • How does online learning affect the parental side of engagement?
  • Does collaboration play a role in education amongst teachers and students?

Key Insights

  • Schools are holding online classes as long as 6 hours straight.
  • A large portion of time gets used up in reading textbooks, following the same sage-on-stage method as before.
  • It is hard for students to focus clearly on the lesson.
  • Whatsapp is the primary tool for discussions, assignments and feedback.
  • The ability to cheat during examinations has become the new norm, sometimes with dear ones assisting.

The Opportunity

Exploring Ideas

After understanding the users & problems deeper, I wrote down areas to focus on to help me think of different ways that could help teachers.

Idea 01

Engagement insights of all students.

“The teacher has appointed students to check attendance at the beginning at the end, or they call us out and check if we are aware. If someone drops out in the meantime, the teacher can not do much.” -Shimon Singhal, student (VIII grade), DPS Rewari

The teacher should have informed insights into their students’ interest and performance. If performance is found lacking, it can be solved. This will allow teachers to track the students with varied learning needs.

Idea 02

An educator community to encourage collaboration.

“School administrations are realizing that finishing school syllabus can't be their solitary goal. Students need to be aware of the topics taught to them and should be able to discuss them in conversations. You can't ask them straightforward questions in examinations." -Ravindra Yatagiri, VP E-learning, UPES

Teachers take the effort and time to browse through tons of reference books to find relevant and innovative teaching methods. It would help to check how a concept is taught by other instructors. With better teaching methods, time will not be wasted online on reading from textbooks. This can be replaced with intentional doubt-reducing sessions or quick quizzes once the students are done reading the lesson once by themselves.

Idea 03

Receiving assignments and give feedback.

“Checking assignments is a huge challenge for me. I can print the work, mark it, click a picture, and send it back. Or I can correct it right in the PDF. Schools should provide graphic tablets for the required annotation, and few are. Most of the teachers are making do with giving feedback separately if they can.”- Neera Kapoor, teacher, Welhams' Girls
  • Are teachers able to point out exactly where the problem exists in an assignment?
  • What is their preferred method of receiving assignments?
  • What is the set of tools they are using to communicate with students, parents and peers?

Whatsapp is being largely used to form groups of students with subject teachers and share assignment requirements. The PDFs with answers are shared over individual chat for discretion. The lack of grouping chats in Whatsapp by purpose can make this experience quite chaotic. The teachers don’t have the time or the patience to compile the work and check, and they miss out on annotating every answer script.

Sketching & Wireframing

I explore potential layouts and wireframes for introducing the product to teachers by creating sketches. They evolved into an application that functions as a one-stop solution for management and communication.

Testing & Iterating

Once I explored different ideas, I wanted to test them with the interviewed teachers. I did qualitative testing with a few screens to try variations.

User Flow

Laying out the user flow enables me to see the big picture. As I build a journey map, I like to focus on potential opportunities.

Schedule

With Sehpath, teachers have their space to manage schedule, jot dot top-of-the-head thoughts, and classify their classes as groups.

Assignments Sharing

Templates to design various types of assignments on the go. The assignments shared earlier are kept handy in the app.

Community

With Sehpath, teachers can check the best of their community in action. They can learn and share insights on what works and implement suitable methods in their classes.

Engagement

Teachers can check how engaged their students are. They can share announcements, assignments and alerts. They can keep track of assignments and if they remain to be corrected.

Annotation

Correcting assignments online comes with its own sets of problems. Digital annotation tools can help avoid typing the feedback for numerous PDFs, making it hassle-free.

Feedback:

After making some changes to the design based on testing, I presented my prototypes to the interviewed teachers and got the following responses:

    “I would be more comfortable using a desktop for marking the papers with a mouse, but this works too.”- Neera Kapoor
    “I usually had big trouble not being able to access my notes in the office. It was tough to compile them all over again.”- Pika Gupta

Based on the feedback, I created a space to manage resources for the teachers. Having book PDFs at hand will make it easier to share the screen during readings (instead of aiming a camera at the book) and copy/paste snippets from books for students to get guidance.

Next Steps & Considerations:

  • Creation of discussion forums for collaborative learning and instant feedback.
  • Annotation is far more convenient on desktop, with advanced functionalities. The mobile app could complement the web.
  • In what way can content creation and innovation be encouraged that appeals to the user base? Can gamification lend itself as a motivation for active digital use?
  • Need to explore methods of measuring teacher performance and insights beyond student scores.
