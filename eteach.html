The Problem

With the newfound need for learning online, teachers and students are adapting to the tools at hand. Teaching online comes with its separate set of issues and schools have recently faced the need to re-skill their education staff.

The Solution

A Learning System at hand that helps in taking an overview of the classes, and provides teachers methods to compensate for real- time insights.

Forming a Hypothesis

I believe there is a massive disconnect between teachers and students, and also amongst teachers themselves.

User Interviews

It was time to interview teachers, students and parents to understand use cases and implications. During the interviews, I asked questions like-

  • “How do you track the presence and engagement of students?”
  • “What are the teaching methods you utilise to get your points across?”
  • “How do you manage and give feedback on assignments of students?”
  • “How active are your classes with extracurricular side projects?”
  • “Do you encourage group activities?”
  • “Do the students listen to you?”

Key Insights

  • Schools are holding online classes as long as 6 hours straight.
  • A major part of these classes is wasted in reading textbooks, following the same sage-on-stage method as before.
  • It is hard for students to focus clearly on the lesson.
  • Whatsapp has formed a major tool for discussions, assignments, feedback and what not.
  • The ability to cheat during examinations has become the new norm, with students uploading videos on Youtube of them cheating with live camera on

The Opportunity

Based on my research, I feel confident that there is a recognizable amount of gap that needs to be mended for conscious teaching. Teachers are now expected to be more creative, interesting even, and judicious with the time.

Design Goals

Sharing Insights

Help teachers get an overview of attendance by the net time any student attends their classes.

Ease of Use

The solution should be seamless and easy to use.

Exploring Ideas

After understanding the users & problems deeper, I wrote down areas to focus on to help me think of different ways that could help teachers. I also went through multiple revisions of different "How Might We" questions to help me have a clear understanding of the problem I was solving.

HMW Question:

How might we ensure that the teachers are able to engage with the students better?

Things to Consider

To get out of the quick ideation mindset I write down 'Things to consider.' When I break the idea down and question its value, I can often find small issues that need to be addressed. Here are a few of those thoughts.

  • What is the current and correct method of engaging students?
  • How can assignments become more exciting for students and teachers, rather than a chore?
  • Is there a way for teachers to mark assignments promptly and effectively?

Idea 01

Incorporating the engagement statistics of all direct students.

The teacher should have informed insights of their students’ interest and performance. If something is found lacking, a problem identification meeting can be organised with parents and the counselor. Not only will this allow for teachers to track the students with varied needs, but also the tracking of attendance, which is a pain to track manually.

Idea 02

Curated database of techniques to explain/ demonstrate various topics.

Effort & time is required for browsing through tons of reference books to find relevant and innovative teaching methods. As a teacher, it would help if I could check how a concept is taught by the best instructors. With better teaching methods, time will not be wasted online on incomprehensive reading from textbooks. This can be replaced with intentional doubt- reducing sessions or quick quizzes once the kids are done reading the lesson once by themselves. Not only will this help us talk about what really matters in a classroom, it will help reduce our screen-times.

Idea 03

Empower teachers to be able to receive assignments and give feedback better.

  • Are teachers able to point out exactly where the problem exists in an assignment?
  • What is their preferred method of receiving assignments?
  • What is the set of tools they are using to communicate with students, parents and peers?

Whatsapp is being largely used to form groups of students with subject teachers and share assignment requirements. The PDFs with answers are shared over individual chat for discretion. The lack of grouping chats in Whatsapp by purpose can make this experience quite chaotic. The teachers don’t have the time or the patience to compile the work and check, and they miss out on annotating every answer script.

Idea 04

Building suggestive kits to ensure engagement during tests and reduce chances of cheating.

  • Teachers should give objective questions only for quick quizzes.
  • If any student drops out in the duration, they should be tested rapidly with different set of questions.
  • For tests, students should be given questions that test their analytical skills rather than memory. Objective questions should be tested in a quick quiz or upfront in video conference calls.
  • Students should be asked to keep their cameras on. If that is not possible, the quantity of questions should be increased, and students should be marked relatively rather than absolutely.
  • Later perhaps, with the help of a question database, teachers could know how unique their question sets are.

Sketching & Wireframing

During sketching, I was able to explore potential layouts and wireframes for introducing the product for teachers. As I sketched, I determined that there can be an app which functions as a one-stop solution for learning management and communication.

Testing & Iterating

Once I explored different ideas, I wanted to test this idea with interviewed teachers. I did some qualitative testing with a few screens to test some variations. I then mocked up screens in Sketch to build my prototype in Invision.

Research Findings:

  • Teachers think creating and annotating assignments can be best accomplished with desktop and a mouse.
  • Teachers feel that it is the instant manner of receiving a whatsapp assignment that makes it valuable for tests, and also because of the wide acceptance of the app.
  • Some teachers were not appreciative of being recorded, and would like to have them deleted from the database, whereas some suggested these could be shared with the underprivileged.

User Flow

Laying out the user flow enables me to see the big picture. As I build a journey map, I like to focus on potential opportunities.

Next Steps & Considerations:

  • Annotation and grading is far more convenient on desktop, with advanced functionalities. The mobile app could complement the web.
  • How does gamification fit in the system to encourage teachers to be more active digitally?
  • In what way can content creation and innovation be incentivized that appeals to the user base?
  • Need to explore methods of measuring teacher performance and insights beyond student scores.
