Group Food Ordering

Project Scope:

  • My Role: User Research, Product Thinking, Interaction Design
  • Tools: Sketch, Pen and Paper
  • Timeline: 27-29 July 2020
  • Deliverables: Research, User Flow, Wireframes, Hypothesis
  • Team: Me, myself and I

The Problem

Food is meant for sharing. When our friends catch up, we hate to meet and merely eat. We have all had get togethers where we spend one hour discussing food choices and then compromise. Food gets there in a while and is ravished. It soon gets time for everyone to leave. What happened was in the moment, and you'll never have the opportunity to ask for money you owe.

Storyboard of the problem

The Solution

A new feature that will fit to the existing app and allow a group of users to order food from the same restaurant using their own devices.

User Interviews

After looking through the apps, I felt that it was time to interview a few power food app users to understand use cases. I talked to 5 'food committee heads'. During the interviews, I asked questions like “Tell me about your experience using Lime,” “What motivates you to take up the project?” “What do you go about doing when you plan these meals?” and “Do you split the bill equally or as per individual order? Is that because deividing it according to individual orders is time consuming?” and also "Do you always get your money back in full? If yes, how?!"

User Quotes:

“I hardly, if ever, get my money back. It's a lot of responsibility with few returns. I always mess up!”
“I wish I didn't have to pass my phone around as a chalkboard for everyone to ponder on the menu.”

Key Insights

  • The device is often passed around to share the menu until one person opens the restaurant menu on their phone.
  • Ordering from multiple restaurants is expensive and inconvenient, and management is preferred on larger screens.
  • Combined vegetarian and non-vegetarian orders can cause issues.
  • Once the food arrives, re-division of the food takes time too.
  • It is a contri conflict when someone chooses a pricier item.
  • Host is responsible for tracking the order.
  • Splitting the bill is an issue.
  • There is a significant money returning issue when one person pays. Getting back the amount requires sending Splitwise or Google Pay requests and some audacity.
  • People prefer to decide on the restaurant before deciding guests.
  • Food experimentation does not happen in larger groups, people like to be sure of the quality.

The Opportunity

With research, I feel there is significant potential in simplifying group orders and reducing the conflict.

Choice of Platform

Although planners often use website for bulk orders from multiple restaurants, making the process available on mobile will be easier to track while commuting.

According to my survey, I realized that bulk ordering is easier with Swiggy's simpler UI and lucrative discounts for promotions. I have chosen that as the base for my project.

I have tried to keep the typeface and layout similar to what Swiggy is currently using.

Exploring Ideas

After understanding the users & problem deeper, I wrote down as many ideas as possible (even the crazy ones) to help me think of different ways to solve this problem. I also went through multiple revisions of different "How Might We" questions to help to me have a clear understanding of the problem I was solving.

HMW Question:

How might we order food for groups as we like and with as little stress as possible?

After generating a few ideas, I selected 3 ideas to explore in more detail. I assume the host to be the primary user and invitees to be secondary users. Below I talk about the pros & cons of each idea:

Idea 01

Have an open chat in the system, where people can join and order.

Pros

This idea would build a sense of community for users, and mirrors real-life scenario.

Cons

This design is not flexible enough for larger groups, and serves intimate groups better.

Idea 02

Suggest restaurants and cuisines based on previous orders of the invited friends!

Pros

Is great for scenarios when you do not know what type of food an invitee likes.

Cons

Is independent of current mood, and highly dependent on members being active users to recognize their tastes. Swiggy might have to incorporate personal food preferences in the profile section so that the selection is easier.

Idea 03

A more flexible approach where you share link with members on another chat, with customizable order limits, payment scenarios and delivery.

Pros

The solution can be used for houseparties and corporate lunches that need to be planned in advance.

Cons

This idea will take time to implement, along with a lot of A/B testing to see what solution works for the demographic.

Why I went with Idea 03?

I feel like there are a few main value propositions for the business and the end customer:

  • Flexibility  —  with the ability to pay the whole or get paid upfront, take orders only util a particular time or not, and maybe set order limits- this solution works for friends as well as corporate lunches. With this, I hope to increase customer satisfaction with personalized offerings.
  • Profit maximization  —  through increment in the variable amount charged from the restaurant, Swiggy will be able to increase profits despite discounts.
  • Product differentiation  —  through creating a better way to host meals, this feature will help separate Swiggy from other products in the market.

Things to Consider

To get out of the quick ideation mindset I write down 'Things to consider.' This way, I can often find small issues that need to be addressed or increase the scope of the problem. Here are a few of those questions:'

  • Do you want to set a limit per person or for the entire group, split equally?
  • Creation of a recurring permanent link to avoid sending invites eg. for recurring kitty parties?
  • Give host the functionality to allow invitees to invite others?- which I later decided against. Better to have clarity when a feature starts out.
  • Should host be able to add individual orders for others if they are not able to?

In the second interaction 2). the user will be notified with a pop-up once they are within the 0.5 mile radius of the scooter.

Testing & Iterating

Once I explored different ideas, I wanted to test this idea with prospective users. I did some qualitative testing with a few screens to test some variations. I then mocked up screens in Sketch to build my prototype in XD.

Research Findings:

  • Invitees need a considerable window of time to consider their options before finalization.
  • In large groups, there should be feedback for the host to know if everyone has placed their orders. If not, there should be a functionality to send reminders.
  • To avoid delivery fee, some people prefer the functionality of pickup to get warm food.
  • Chat functionality is liked between small and intimate groups, but restraints might have to be built in to maintain context for the placement of order.

Usability Issue :

The main usability issue that I found was that students will have issues ordering in advance, and there will be delays in individual payments.

Design Decision:

To help with singular payments and easy money splitting, Swiggy payments could be done directly with Swiggy money for instant payments. If an invitee is perchance not a user of Swiggy, the Swiggy amount could be requested via Google Pay or Splitwise. This would allow the users to manage the payment post get-together as well.
