Paytm for all Visions

Project Scope:

  • My Role: User research, Product thinking
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Indesign
  • Timeline: Jan- April 2019 (16 weeks)
  • Deliverables: Research findings, presentation, document
  • Team: Me, myself and I

The Problem

Demonetization was harsher on the visually impaired- the new currency offers them no clue as to what value they are handing out. The sizes of the notes are similar, and to distinguish amongst them is haptically difficult.

Forming a Hypothesis

Gap analysis of the Indian fintech industry led me to the communities which suffer from increased payment friction.

I went through multiple revisions of different "How Might We" questions to help me have a clear understanding of the problem I was solving.

HMW Question:

How might we reduce the financial dependence of the visually impaired?

User Research

When I developed this hypothesis, I asked myself the following: what kind of data do I need to test this hypothesis? Where will I find users to validate my ideas?


I went to various organizations to find out if the visually impaired were financially dependent. I interviewed around 45 research participants- the visually impaired, accessibility experts and NGO administrators. I found that many negative reviews mentioned this problem.

User Quotes:

“I asked my friend to teach me how to use a credit card because I can't use cash.”
“I used Paytm when I was partially blind, but it worsens with every update.”

Key Insights

  • Visually Impaired customers prefer PhonePe and GooglePay to Paytm.
  • The app interface changes often.
  • KYC is a hurdle in smooth onboarding. The visually impaired still using Paytm have been using it due to the ease of agent visit available earlier.
  • A one-click-to-reach system is not suitable for easy 1-D navigation. Categorization is preferred.
  • Typing PIN is risky. There are different methods visually impaired use to ensure safety.
  • Users find it hard to customize layout, content and structure.

The Opportunity

PWD have difficulty using a brick-and-mortar bank or credit union because of travel barriers or time restrictions.


Digital technology has the potential to be a great equalizer. Mobile financial services are a convenient option, anytime and anyplace. If not accessible, technology only further excludes people with disabilities from engaging with a brand.


Customers with disabilities provide a large, valuable market currently underserved by the financial services industry. The visually Impaired population is the fastest-growing minority in India. Banks and credit unions cannot afford to ignore the opportunity to create relations with this untapped market. Limited financial knowledge has resulted in an unstable relationship between people with disabilities and financial institutions.

Design Goals

Build Trust

Help customers feel confident paying and receiving money without relying on others.

Ease of Use

The solution should be seamless and easy to use.

Persona Spectrums

When we solve for accessibility, we all benefit. Solving for visual impairments can help many.

Personas are an average of attributes that we imagine our average customer has. For this project, I forsook the creation of personas. When it comes to accessibility, everyone has created their pathways and mental models. An average persona ceases to exist.

Disability is situational. The inability/ disability to interact with an interface may depend on the context. For example, a person might have a permanent condition due to being blind since birth. They could also be recovering from eye surgery and have limited or no vision. Another person might face this while operating a device during the daytime under the glare of the sun.

Different people with similar needs. By gathering real insights from real people who share the motivations we are designing for, we can start understanding and incorporating the full range of human diversity.

Exploring Ideas

After understanding the users & problem deeper, I wrote down as many ideas as possible to help me think of different ways to solve this problem.

Accessibility Testing

RESEARCH METHOD 1

Manual Accessibility Tests

By interacting with your Paytm consumer app using accessibility services, I could experience the app like the visually impaired users. TalkBack/ VoiceOver are built-in screen readers. When turned on, users can interact with their device without seeing the screen.

TESTING METHOD 2

Scanning Accessibility Tests

Testing with analysis tools can uncover opportunities to improve accessibility that you might miss with manual testing.

TESTING METHOD 3

User Testing

I stepped out of the building! Nothing beats testing with real users on the field and getting feedback on our app. NIVH, BRA and NAB are some organizations where you can find people with visual impairments at work.

Accessibility Rulebook

Implementing accessibility long after building a complex product is an intricate process and requires a well-structured plan. I created guidelines and shared them with the team to ensure a smooth transition into an accessible platform.. The guidelines comprise a set of design and development audit checklists for quality assurance.
