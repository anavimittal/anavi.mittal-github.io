Paytm for all Visions

Project Scope:

  • My Role: User research, Product thinking
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Indesign
  • Timeline: Jan- April 2019 (16weeks)
  • Deliverables: research findings, presentation, document
  • Team: Me, myself and I

The Problem

Demonetization was harsher on the visually impaired- the new currency offers them no clue as to what value they are handing out. The sizes of the notes are similar, and to distinguish amongst them isn't haptically easy.

Forming a Hypothesis

Gap analysis of the Indian fintech industry led me to the communities which suffer from increased payment friction, and I found immense growth potential there.
Before diving into any problem, I believe it’s important to state a problem hypothesis and then test it. Testing a problem hypothesis allows me to develop a deep understanding of the problem, discover implications, and check how it has been solved. In this case, I realized that many people never questioned the financial problems of the visually impaired, and I felt it was important to find the answer.

My Hypothesis

People with severe visual impairments are unable to use Paytm properly due to lack of accessible design and development.

User Research

When I developed this hypothesis, I asked myself the following: what kind of data do I need to test this hypothesis? Where will I find users to validate my ideas?


I went to the various organizations to find out if the visually impaired were financially dependent. I interviewed around 45 research participants including the visually impaired, accessibility experts and NGO administrators. and found that many negative reviews mentioned this problem.

User Quotes:

“I asked my friend to teach me how to use credit card because I can't use cash.”
“I used Paytm when I was partially blind, par vo har update ke saath bura hota jata hai.”

Key Insights

  • Visually Impaired customers prefer PhonePe and GooglePay to Paytm.
  • Interface of the app changes often.
  • KYC is a major hurdle in smooth onboarding. The visually impaired still using Paytm have been using it due to the ease of agent visit available earlier.
  • One-click-to-reach system is not suitable to an understandable 1-D navigation. Categorization is preferred.
  • Typing PIN is risky and there are different methods visually impaired use to ensure safety.
  • Users find it hard to customize layout, content and structure.

The Opportunity

PWD have difficulty using a bricks-and-mortar bank or credit union because of travel barriers or time restrictions.

Digital technology has the potential to be a great equalizer. Mobile financial services are a convenient “anytime, anyplace” option. If not accessible, technology only further excludes people with disabilities from engaging with a brand.

Customers with disabilities provide a large and valuable market that is currently underserved by the financial services industry. Visually Impaired population is the largest growing minority in India. Creating sustainable, long-term relationships with this large, untapped market is an opportunity that banks and credit unions can no longer afford to ignore. Limited financial knowledge has resulted in an unstable relationship between people with disabilities and financial institutions.

Design Goals

Build Trust

Help customers feel confident paying and receiving money without relying on others.

Ease of Use

The solution should be seamless and easy to use.

Persona Spectrums

When we solve for accessibility, we all benefit. Solving for visual impairments can help many.

For this project I forsook the creation of personas, which are an average of attributes that we imagine our average customer has. When it comes to accessibility, everyone has created their own pathways and mental models. An average persona doesn't exist.

Disability is situational. The inability/ disability to interact with an interface may depend on the context. For example, a person might have a permanent condition due to being blind since birth. They could also be recovering from eye surgery and have limited or no vision. Another person might face this barrier in certain environments, like operating a device during daytime under the glare of the sun.

Different people, similar needs. By gathering real insights from real people who share the motivations we are designing for, we can start understanding and incorporating the full range of human diversity, designing for individuals and their aspirations.

Exploring Ideas

After understanding the users & problem deeper, I wrote down as many ideas as possible to help me think of different ways to solve this problem. I also went through multiple revisions of different "How Might We" questions to help to me have a clear understanding of the problem I was solving.

HMW Question:

How might we reduce financial dependence of the visually impaired?

Accessibility Testing

RESEARCH METHOD 1

Manual Accessibility Tests

By interacting with your Paytm consumer app using accessibility services, I was able to experience the app as visually impaired users would. TalkBack/ VoiceOver are built-in screen readers. When turned on, users can interact with their device without seeing the screen.

TESTING METHOD 2

Scanning Accessibility Tests

Testing with analysis tools can uncover opportunities to improve accessibility that you might miss with manual testing.

TESTING METHOD 3

User Testing

Let’s get out of the building, nothing beats testing with real users on the field and getting feedback on our app. NIVH, BRA and NAB are some organizations where you can find people with visual impairments at work.

Accessibility Rulebook

Implimenting accessibility long after the creation of a complex system is an intricate process, and requires a well- structured plan. To ensure a smooth transition of the Paytm platforms into an accessible platform, guidelines were created and shared with the team. The guidelines comprise a set of design and development audit checklists for quality assurance.
